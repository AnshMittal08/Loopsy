# StitchFlow AI — Product Vision

## What We Are Building

StitchFlow AI is an AI-native crochet project studio — the first platform that serves both **makers** (people who crochet) and **designers** (people who write and sell patterns) in one cohesive product. We are not a pattern archive. We are not a generic AI tool. We are the intelligent layer that the $6 billion global yarn crafts market has never had.

The positioning in one line:

## Marketplace Mechanics

### Commission structure

```
Free user selling:     20% platform commission
Maker Pro selling:     12% platform commission
Creator selling:        8% platform commission
```

A designer earning $500/month saves $60/month by upgrading to Creator ($18). The upgrade pays for itself 3x over. Incentive is built into the structure.

### Pattern quality validation — key differentiator

Before a pattern can list, the AI runs:
- Stitch count verification at every row
- Structure coherence check (do the parts fit together)
- Materials completeness check

Patterns that pass receive a **"Verified" badge**. No other marketplace offers this. Buyers trust verified patterns. Designers with verified badges sell more. Both sides win.

### Pricing guidance built-in

Platform suggests pricing based on complexity, category, and comparable patterns:
- Beginner patterns: $3–5
- Intermediate: $5–8
- Advanced: $8–15

New designers consistently undercharge because they have no reference. We solve this.

### Designer trust — the critical community policy

The crochet design community is deeply protective of their IP. Ravelry's community has been vocal about AI fears. If we do not address this directly, designers will not list on our platform and the marketplace never starts.

**The policy:**
> AI-generated patterns on StitchFlow AI are for **personal use only**. They cannot be listed or sold in the marketplace. Only human-designed patterns can be sold. AI generation is never trained on marketplace patterns without explicit designer opt-in consent.

This creates a clean separation:
- **Makers** use AI for personal creative exploration (subscription revenue)
- **Designers** sell human-crafted patterns (marketplace revenue)
- No conflict between the two audiences

Make this policy prominent on every designer-facing page. The crochet community talks — this reputation spreads.

## Feature Differentiators — What Nobody Else Has

### 1. Photo → Pattern (biggest moat, Phase 3)
Upload a photo of any crocheted item. Claude Vision analyzes the stitch structure, counts rows, identifies techniques, and reverse-engineers a crochet pattern. **Nobody does this.** Technically feasible with Claude's vision API today. This feature alone would go viral in every crochet community, blog, and subreddit. This is the "wow moment" that drives organic growth.

### 2. Yarn Stash Manager (Phase 3)
Photograph a yarn label → Claude reads it (brand, weight, yardage, fibre content, colour) → adds to digital stash automatically. Then: "What can I make from what I have?" — AI matches stash to patterns in the template library and marketplace. Ravelry has a manual version of this from 2008. An AI-first stash-to-pattern recommender is a meaningful daily-use retention feature.

### 3. Modular Crochet Design Studio (Phase 3)
A visual character designer where users assemble items from parts rather than describing them in text. For amigurumi: pick head shape, body shape, ear type, limb style, size. An SVG preview assembles in real time. When user clicks Generate, Claude receives structured design parameters and produces a pattern for those exact proportions. This produces significantly more accurate patterns than freeform prompts because the math is constrained by the design.

**Why SVG not 3D:** 3D shapes look abstract and don't represent the finished item recognizably. SVG flat illustrations in a crochet-native art style load instantly, work on all devices, and feel native to the crafting aesthetic. A library of ~20 SVG components (round head, oval head, floppy ear, pointed ear, etc.) can be assembled into thousands of combinations.

### 4. Stitch Diagram Generation (Phase 4)
Convert any text pattern into an interactive SVG stitch diagram — the symbol charts used by professional designers. US/UK crochet terminology differs, but stitch diagrams are universal. Auto-generating them from text patterns is currently done by hand by human designers and is extremely time-consuming. Being the first platform to automate this would be a significant competitive advantage.

### 5. Skill-Adaptive AI (Phase 3)
Track every project a user completes. Build a skill profile: knows magic rings, granny squares, amigurumi, but hasn't tried cables or lace. Next suggestions stretch the skill slightly — like a personal trainer. Generated patterns are calibrated to that specific user's demonstrated level, not just a generic difficulty label.

### 6. Real-Time Cost Calculator (Phase 2)
Enter yarn cost per skein. As user browses patterns or tracks a project, see estimated material cost and spend-to-date. Crochet gifters care deeply about this. Nobody shows it. Simple to build, high daily utility.

## AI Strategy

### Capability honesty — what Claude can and cannot do

**Reliably good:**
- Simple geometric shapes (scarves, dishcloths, basic hats, simple bags)
- Correct crochet terminology and pattern structure
- Metadata (hook size, yarn weight, materials, time estimates)
- Pattern instructions in plain, readable English

**Unreliable:**
- Stitch count arithmetic in complex patterns (counts drift after ~20 steps)
- Structural integrity (has never physically crocheted anything)
- Precise gauge-based sizing for fitted garments
- Complex construction with multiple shaped pieces
- Truly novel shapes without training data precedent

**Accuracy estimate by project type:**

| Pattern type | Accuracy if actually crocheted |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Scarf, dishcloth, simple square | 75–85% |
| Basic hat, simple bag | 55–70% |
| Basic amigurumi sphere shapes | 50–65% |
| Fitted garment with sizing | 20–35% |
| Complex lace or cables | 15–25% |

**Positioning implication:** Never claim "AI-perfect patterns instantly." Position AI generation as a creative exploration and starting-point tool. The template library (human-designed, mathematically verified) is the reliable path. AI is the "experiment and imagine" path.

### AI cost management

Claude Sonnet: ~$0.006–0.01 per generation
At 1,000 users/day generating 2 patterns each: ~$12–20/day

**Cost controls:**

1. **Prompt caching** — system prompt is identical every call. Add `cache_control: ephemeral` to the system prompt in aiService.js. Cuts 90% of repeated system prompt token costs. Implement immediately.

2. **Tiered model selection** — use Claude Haiku for Beginner patterns (10x cheaper), Sonnet only for Intermediate and Advanced. 80% of requests route to Haiku.

3. **Freemium gate** — 3 free generations/month. Users who hit the limit and love the product convert. Subscription revenue covers all AI costs at modest scale.

4. **Rate limiting** — 3 AI generations per hour per session. Prevents abuse without affecting legitimate users.

5. **Prompt-level caching** — hash the user prompt + difficulty. If 50 people request "basic amigurumi bear beginner" in the same day, serve a cached result. Significant savings at scale.

### RAG pipeline — the path to better accuracy

The core accuracy problem: Claude invents stitch counts from memory instead of deriving them from proven patterns.

**The fix — a three-layer knowledge system:**

```
LAYER 1: Math Constants DB (immediate, free)
  Sphere formulas, hat sizing tables, amigurumi proportion
  ratios, gauge conversion tables. Hardcoded in system prompt.
  No scraping needed. Raises accuracy floor immediately.

LAYER 2: Pattern Vector DB (Phase 2, 1 week of work)
  22 existing templates + curated CC-licensed patterns
  embedded with Voyage AI + stored in sqlite-vec.
  Retrieve 2-3 similar patterns as context when generating.
  Claude pattern-matches against correct math instead of guessing.

LAYER 3: Community Patterns (Phase 3, self-improving)
  User-created patterns rated 4+ stars added to vector DB
  with consent. Platform generates its own training data
  over time. Compound advantage grows with every user.
```

**Implementation stack:**
- Embeddings: Voyage AI (Anthropic partner, clean integration)
- Vector storage: `sqlite-vec` — extends existing SQLite, zero new infrastructure
- No Pinecone, no Postgres, no new cloud services

**Data sourcing — what is and is not legal:**

| Source | Legal status |
|--------|-------------|
| Ravelry | Explicitly prohibits scraping in ToS. Do not use. |
| LoveCrafts | Same. Copyright protected. |
| Most pattern blogs | Copyright of designer. Infringement risk. |
| Creative Commons patterns | Safe. Search GitHub for CC-licensed pattern repos. |
| Your own platform's patterns | You own this. Best long-term source. |
| Commissioned original patterns | Pay a crochet pattern writer for 100 verified patterns. ~$2,000–5,000. Zero legal risk. High quality. This is the right investment. |

## Beginner Experience — Learn as You Make

### Why beginners are the highest-value acquisition segment

Every experienced crocheter was once a beginner. The platform they learned on is the platform they stay on. **Ravelry assumes you already know how to crochet.** Not one major crochet platform has built a genuine learn-as-you-make experience. That gap is our primary acquisition engine.

The beginner flywheel:
```
Beginner joins to learn
        ↓
Uses AI to get a simple first pattern
        ↓
Completes first project (the emotional peak — they made something)
        ↓
Gains confidence, tries harder patterns
        ↓
Hits the free AI limit → upgrades to Maker Pro
        ↓
Eventually writes their own patterns → lists in marketplace → Creator tier
```

A user who completes their first project on StitchFlow associates the platform with their own achievement. That is the strongest form of retention. Users who learn here do not leave.

### Feature 8 — "What Went Wrong?" Troubleshooting (Phase 3)

The second biggest beginner drop-off point is "something looks wrong and I don't know why." A visual troubleshooting guide using symptom → diagnosis → fix format:

- Edges getting wider or narrower → tension or missed stitches at row ends
- Holes appearing in solid fabric → accidentally working into chain spaces
- Rounds spiraling visibly → not marking the start of rounds
- Magic ring won't close → tail not pulled tight before first round
- Pieces won't join cleanly → tension mismatch between pieces

Long-term: let users photograph their problem and ask Claude Vision what went wrong. Phase 3 feature, but the static guide ships in Phase 2 at near-zero cost.

### How beginners change the business model

Beginner users have measurably longer lifetime value in craft platforms because the platform is associated with a skill they are proud of learning. Duolingo's retention data shows exactly this — users who associate a platform with their own growth are extremely difficult to churn.

The conversion trigger is the moment a beginner completes their first project. At that moment they feel capable, they feel proud, and they want to make more. If the AI generation limit is the next step, a meaningful percentage will upgrade immediately — not because they were pushed, but because they are motivated.

This is the acquisition mechanic no current crochet platform has built. It is also the most defensible moat: **you cannot easily copy the relationship between a beginner and the platform where they learned**.

Crochet alone has a realistic ceiling of $5–10M ARR. To build beyond that:

### 1. Add knitting (immediate 3–4x TAM)
Ravelry serves both markets. Knitting has ~3x the participants of crochet. The tracker, AI generation, marketplace, and stash manager all transfer directly. Same codebase, different stitch vocabulary. This is the single highest-leverage expansion move.

### 2. All fiber arts
Embroidery, cross-stitch, weaving, needlepoint. All use patterns. All lack modern tooling. Same product architecture, different domain vocabularies. Each adds a meaningful audience segment.

### 3. Global localization
Current numbers cover English-speaking markets only. Crochet is enormous in Brazil (Portuguese), Germany, Netherlands, and Japan. Localization unlocks parallel markets at relatively low incremental cost.

### 4. B2B yarn brand integrations
Yarn companies (Lion Brand, Paintbox, Drops) spend significantly on content marketing. Opportunities:
- Sponsored pattern collections ("50 patterns using Lion Brand Pound of Love")
- Co-branded AI generation ("generate a pattern optimized for bulky yarn")
- Yarn store inventory integration (customers see what's in stock at their local store)

## The Long-Term Vision

Build the **Duolingo of fiber arts** — not a social feed, not a content archive, but an intelligent creative tool that helps people make things with their hands. Focused on learning, creating, and progressing as a maker. Deeply personalized. Genuinely useful every single day someone picks up a hook or needle.

Ravelry proved the market exists and that it sustains a highly profitable business. It just stopped evolving 15 years ago. The window to build the modern version is open now — the Gen Z and Millennial crochet wave brought millions of new digitally-native makers who have never had a tool built for them.

That is what StitchFlow AI is for.
